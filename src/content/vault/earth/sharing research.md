title: sharing research

- sharing as you go
- documentation for reflection, for archival or for sharing?
- documentation often a iterative, internal, process driven
- sharing just a version that is more active and public-facing

sharing research to:-
- explain
- archive in the commons
- reflect, challenge or provoke
- clarify your thinking
- help you understand what matters
- connect with your community
- find wider audience, who else might benefit
- present in different ways to diff groups
- learn from others

connectivity of ideas
	• invite dialogue, challenge, expand, or support your ideas
	• contribute to a wider conversation  - research doesn’t live in isolation
	• your process may help others find their own path

exemplary sharing modes
- curated/static/reflective
	- blog style
	- Exhibition
	- Written documentation
	- Papers, articles
	- Posters, [[zine cultures|zines]], etc
	- Walkthrough videos
- live/in progress
	- Performance
	- Wiki-style docs
	- [[digital gardens]]
	- [[micro-content]]
	- Livestreams

- more innovative methods exist now online
- novel formats
- blurring the boundaries of presented and live
- sharing by default
- [[working in public|working with the garage door up]]
