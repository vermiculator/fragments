title: movement is exercise
---

- destigmatising exercise
- exercise as a basis of trauma for many, forced exercise, starting too intensive, extreme fatphobia, body dysmorphia and dysphoria, post-exertional malaise as a symptom of complex illness, etc
- why do you exercise - to stay alive, to feel comfortable and safe and secure in your body, to carry a child or pet, etc
- active jobs and workplace exercise
- social management movement, sports and dance and similar
- expressive movement, dance and similar
- slow and gentle work on strength and flexibility and balance, eg yoga or tai-chi
- cardio through excitement and joy and games
- sex as exercise
- movement as a casual part of the everyday, integrating it into life more consciously
- environmental affordances that encourage gentle movement, building play into space and varied ways of engaging bodies
- IKEA did a range of everyday workout-adjacent objects that are also just items you'd have around - multi-purpose everyday items :)
- clothes should also facilitate everyday movement, everyday comfies
- old people accessible movement routines, and also for other people with limited mobility. 
- kids - suitable to bodily development 
- calisthenics and 'natural movement' 
- stimming.
