title: "Touchscreens Are Out, and Tactile Controls Are Back"
summary: Touchscreen controls in cars and appliances are being replaced by physical buttons and knobs. This trend, called "re-buttonization," is driven by people craving tactile feedback and experiencing screen fatigue. Experts like Rachel Plotnick are helping companies design better tactile interfaces by learning from the history of buttons.
