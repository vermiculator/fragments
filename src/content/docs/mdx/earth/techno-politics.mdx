* can you define [politics](/mdx/earth/politics)?
* using technology to achieve political goals or to stabilise political orders ^8709eb
* technical infrastructure as forms of order eg
  * NY low bridges prevent buses reaching beach destinations. Robert Moses
    * what happens when these exclusionary structures become beloved, part of place - relates to [colonialist architecture](/mdx/colonialist-architecture)/21st century backlash against visual/cultural evidence of colonial legacies
* [spatial design](/mdx/earth/spatial-design)/histories of architectures and city planning have both implicit and explicit political purpose [spatial design is political](/mdx/spatial-design-is-political)
* [anti-protest measures](/mdx/earth/anti-protest-measures)
* some technology is more explicitly compatible with different political systems eg solar or nuclear, [tech for war](/mdx/earth/tech-for-war) and [tech for good](/mdx/earth/tech-for-good)
* digital technologies, [internet sociology](/mdx/earth/internet-sociology), data inequities, etc
