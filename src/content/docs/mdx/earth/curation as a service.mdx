* must be an inherently relational practise.
* has a specific audience in mind, whether this is an [imagined audience](/mdx/earth/imagined-audiences) or a client.
* if no specific client, is this more of a creative practise, archival practise, a public personal archive, regardless of 'intended' audience?

***

* contemporary example, web-weaving requests on [tumblr](/mdx/entities/tumblr)
* historical example of shipping logs remixed into maps as a curational service, inherently relational, but also exchanges a mass of data as prerequisite - echoes of current big data practise (see [involuntary data capture](/mdx/earth/involuntary-data-capture)) [(Smolicki, 2017)](/mdx/library/para-archives---rethinking-personal-archiving-practices-in-the-times-of-capture-culture#block-c03ea2)
